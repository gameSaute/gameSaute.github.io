Description

In 2014 Sage made a little game about collecting gems and falling pits. The graphics were crude, there was no strategy, and yet Sage found themselves in times of of anxiety and inattention coming back to the game, as would others. They played it thousands of times. The game was a practice of focus to avoid accidentally stepping into one of the pits. The longer you could focus the higher your score would be. It both trained focus and by focusing one was able to temporaily push out all the thoughts of the future in their head and return to present.

Sage wanted to be able to make the game more accessible to other people and share this thing that helps them so much.

When Basil said they wanted to try making a video game with Sage in 2017, The choice was obvious, A remastered version of this game we all love so much. This is that game, Picking Flowers. A game Sage loves remasted with art by someone Sage loves. We hope you enjoy playing as the two of us running around grabbing flowers.

-Sage

Features

  • 3 gameplay modes- Compass, Turn and Analog
  • 6 Competitive Highscore boards
  • The game evolves and changes as you play, can you get to the end?
  • 37 scientificly accurately named flowers to collect and fill your garden with
  • Alternate playing as real life Basil and Sage as they run around collecting flowers.

About Game Sauté

Boilerplate
At Game Sauté we are dedicated to making wholesome and ethically designed original games. Games can have a huge impact on the way players think. All our games are designed around a core positive benefit from playing them. Blazing Tales Inspires you to be more creative. Disaster Diner teaches you how to manage stress better, Picking Flowers asserts on mindfulness. We decide on a wholesome core and design everything else in the game around it.
